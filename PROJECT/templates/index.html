Welcome to Sign Language Recognition

Sign language recognition (SLR) is a technology that enables computers to interpret and understand sign languages used by the deaf and hard-of-hearing community. It combines computer vision, natural language processing, and machine learning to analyze gestures, hand movements, facial expressions, and body posture to accurately translate them into spoken or written language.Bridging the Communication Gap:It allows seamless communication between people with hearing impairments and the rest of society. Increased Accessibility:Enhances accessibility in various sectors like education, healthcare, and customer service.Real-Time Assistance:Offers real-time translation for daily conversations, making communication faster and more effective.
